Asha: Lured away from her village with promises of a good job in the city, Asha found herself trapped in a brothel. She endured unimaginable physical and emotional abuse, but her unwavering spirit kept her going. Asha clung to the memory of her family and her dreams of a better life. She found solace in the companionship of other victims, forming a fragile bond that helped them endure their suffering together.

Ramesh: A young boy from a rural family, Ramesh was tricked into leaving home by a smooth-talking stranger who promised him a job in a big city. Instead, he was forced into child labor in a factory, working long hours in dangerous conditions for little to no pay. Ramesh's resilience was tested to the limit, but he drew strength from his love for his family and his determination to escape his tormentors. He managed to find a moment of opportunity and alerted the authorities, leading to his rescue.

Priya: A bright and ambitious student, Priya was targeted by a human trafficker who promised her a scholarship to study abroad. Upon arrival, she found herself trapped in a forced marriage, isolated from the outside world and subjected to constant abuse. Priya's love for learning and her intellectual curiosity became her lifeline. She secretly taught herself English, using books and scraps of paper she managed to obtain. With the help of a sympathetic teacher, she was able to reach out to a human rights organization and seek their assistance.
